## 3.4 Educational posts — 10 templates

### 31. "What an 'audited' service-charge statement actually proves"
> An "audited" service-charge statement does not prove the OA is well-run. It only proves the numbers reconcile to the bank statements. The two questions that actually matter are different:
> 
> 1. Is the reserve fund being topped up at the rate the OA budget says it is?
> 2. Has the audit ever returned a "qualified" opinion in the last 3 years?
> 
> If the answer to (1) is no, your service charge will spike in the next major works cycle. If (2) is yes, walk away.
> 
> Save this. Before your next purchase.
> 
> `#OAUAE #DubaiBuyerEducation #ServiceCharge #realestateliteracy #propertyfinder`

---

### 32. "Why DOM ≠ overpriced"
> Days-on-market is the most misread number on Bayut. A 90-day DOM does not necessarily mean a property is overpriced. The four real causes of high DOM are: pricing, photography, listing copy, and building reputation. Of these, only one is fixed by a price drop. The other three are fixed by a better agent — and a price drop without fixing them just lowers the close price.
> 
> Save this. Forward to the agent who only knows one move.
> 
> `#realestateadvice #BayutAgent #DubaiSeller #propertyfinderUAE #realestateliteracy`

---

### 33. "What 'freehold' means for non-GCC buyers"
> "Freehold" in Dubai means: you own the unit and the land share, you can sell, lease, mortgage, and inherit it without restriction, regardless of nationality.
> 
> "Freehold" in Sharjah, in most cases, means GCC nationals only — non-GCC buyers operate on a 99-year long-lease structure that is functionally similar but legally distinct. Different transaction process, different mortgage availability.
> 
> Read your title deed. The word "freehold" alone tells you nothing without the next 3 lines.
> 
> `#freeholdUAE #DubaiPropertyLaw #NonGCCBuyer #realestateUAE #اماراتي_عقار`

### 34. "The Golden Visa property thresholds — 2025"
> The current property pathway to a UAE Golden Visa: AED 2M minimum invested in property (cash or mortgage-funded, with the bank confirmation). The threshold can be split across multiple properties.
> 
> What's NOT in the rules but is worth knowing: the visa lasts 10 years, is renewable; you cannot use rental income as the qualifying threshold; the property must be owned outright in your individual name (not a company unless specifically structured).
> 
> Save this. Forward to the friend asking "what's the visa threshold this year?"

> `#goldenvisauae #DubaiGoldenVisa #UAEResidency #UAEinvestor #اقامة_ذهبية`

### 36. "Saudi REGA — what every buyer should check before signing"
> REGA (the Real Estate General Authority) governs every sale in Saudi Arabia. Three things to verify in REGA's portal before you sign anything:
> 
> 1. The seller's authorization to sell (the property must be registered to them or their authorized agent)
> 2. The property's REGA license number, current and active
> 3. Any liens, mortgages, or restrictions registered against the property
> 
> All three checks are public, free, and take 4 minutes total. There is no excuse for skipping them.
> 
> `#REGA #realestateSA #saudirealestate #REGAcompliance #عقارات_السعودية`

### 37. "Service charge variance: how to read it"
> Service charge variance — the difference between this year's charge and the OA's previously declared rate — tells you more about a building's health than the headline number does.
> 
> A 5% YoY variance is normal. 8–12% suggests the OA is catching up on under-funded reserves. 15%+ suggests structural issues (legal claims, deferred maintenance, leadership turnover) — investigate before buying.
> 
> Save this for your next OA budget review.
> 
> `#OAUAE #DubaiOwnersAssociation #servicecharge #propertyfinderUAE #BayutInvestor`

### 38. "The post-handover payment plan — what to actually negotiate"
> Most off-plan post-handover payment plans run 24 months at 1.5% monthly. The numbers worth negotiating are not the headline rate — they're:

> 2. The early-payoff clause (developers often charge 2–5% on early closure; negotiate this to 0)
> 3. The OA service-charge handling during PHP (some developers waive year-1; many don't)

> Save this. Bring it to your next signing meeting.

### 40. "How to read an OA budget in under 10 minutes"
> An OA budget has 4 sections that matter. Open them in this order:
> 
> 1. **Reserve fund balance and YoY change** — is it growing, flat, or declining?
> 2. **Major works schedule (5-year plan)** — what's coming, when, at what cost?
> 3. **Insurance line** — has the premium spiked? (Often a leading indicator of structural issues.)
> 4. **Legal & professional fees** — has this line jumped? (Suggests active disputes.)
> 
> 90% of buyers never open the OA budget. The 10% who do save themselves AED 20–80K over the holding period.
> 
> Save this.
>
