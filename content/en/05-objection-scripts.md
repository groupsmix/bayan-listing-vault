# Section 5 — The 15 WhatsApp Objection Scripts

Text-only. **No phone calls.** Each objection includes:

- **The objection** — in the buyer's likely words
- **The WhatsApp reply** — 1–3 messages
- **2 follow-up variations** — for different buyer pressures
- **Do not say this** — the common bad reply you should avoid

---

## 1. "The price is too high."

**Reply (single WhatsApp):**
> The price is the asking price; the deal is whatever the seller and the buyer agree. On this property, the comparables (3 in the same stack, last 90 days) sit at AED `{{comp_low}}` to `{{comp_high}}`. If your number is inside that band, write it on WhatsApp now, I'll route it to the seller this evening with the comparable transaction set attached.

**Variation A (price-anchored buyer):**
> Your number doesn't have to be the asking. Send the number you'd close at today, in writing. I'd rather route a real offer than discuss a hypothetical one.

**Variation B (skeptical buyer):**
> If "too high" comes from the comparable data, send me the data points you're using. I'll either agree or correct the comparable set with the trustee-system records. Either way you'll have a cleaner number.

**Do not say this:**
> ~~"This is the best price you'll get."~~ ~~"Prices are going up."~~ ~~"You won't find this anywhere else."~~

---

## 2. "We want to wait and see."

**Reply:**
> Wait-and-see is a real strategy — most buyers should run it. Two questions to make it actually work: (1) what number, on what date, would change your mind? (2) which 3 buildings do you want price-tracked weekly while you wait? I'll send a weekly 1-line WhatsApp on those, no other follow-up.

**Variation A (genuinely market-paused):**
> Send me the trigger event — rate cut, school year, exit from current lease — and I'll re-engage on that exact date with a fresh shortlist. Otherwise, parking the file is fine.

**Variation B (uncertain buyer):**
> Wait-and-see usually hides one specific worry. If you're comfortable, name the worry and I'll either resolve it with data or confirm it's the right reason to wait.

**Do not say this:**
> ~~"The market won't wait for you."~~ ~~"This is the dip."~~

---

## 3. "We're still looking around."

**Reply:**
> Good. Looking around is the job. Two questions to keep your file useful on my end: (1) what's the shortlist look like right now (areas, budget, rough timeline), (2) which agent is your primary contact — I'd rather know I'm one of three and act accordingly than pretend I'm the only one.

**Variation A (broad-market buyer):**
> If you want, I can send a weekly market-pulse WhatsApp covering your areas — 3 lines, no pitches. Reply "weekly" and I'll add you. Otherwise we'll talk when you have the shortlist tight.

**Variation B (no real shortlist yet):**
> If the shortlist is still vague, the answer isn't more listings — it's narrower criteria. Try this: max budget all-in, must-haves (3), nice-to-haves (3), deal-breakers (3). Send those nine items, I'll send a 1-page tailored shortlist within 24 hours.

**Do not say this:**
> ~~"You won't find better."~~ ~~"Let me know when you're serious."~~

## 4. "I need to talk to my spouse / family."

**Reply:**
> Of course. Two things to make that conversation more useful: (1) attached, a 1-page summary of the property covering what you and your spouse would each likely care about (numbers and lifestyle), (2) a 5-minute video walkthrough so they see the unit even if they can't visit. Send both whenever you're ready to talk together.

**Variation A (decision-by-committee buyer):**
> If a parent or sibling is part of the decision, I'll send the same pack to them — Arabic or English, your call. Send the WhatsApp number when ready.

**Variation B (genuinely solo decision held up by a partner):**
> If the conversation lands as "yes, with concerns", send me the list of concerns directly. I'll reply with data on each one, in writing, that you can forward.

**Do not say this:**
> ~~"It's a great deal — they'll see that."~~ ~~"Don't take too long."~~

## 5. "Your agent style is too pushy."

**Reply:**
> Fair feedback. I won't follow up unless you message first. If you want, I'll add you to a once-a-month market-pulse WhatsApp at 3 lines max. Reply "monthly" or "stop following up" — I'll do exactly that.

**Variation A (buyer self-reflecting):**
> If a specific message felt pushy, tell me which one — genuine feedback makes me a better agent. Either way, your file stays open and I'll re-engage only when you say.

**Variation B (buyer testing the agent):**
> The pace of follow-up is the buyer's choice, always. From now on: no message from me unless you message first.

**Do not say this:**
> ~~"I was just trying to help."~~ ~~"This is how the market works."~~

## 6. "I'm worried about off-plan handover risk."

**Reply:**
> The risk is real, not theoretical. Three things that protect you specifically: (1) the RERA escrow account — your money sits in escrow until milestones are met, (2) the developer's published phased-handover schedule (attached for this project), (3) the contractual remedy clause for delay beyond 12 months past the contracted handover. If you want, I'll send the developer's last 3 handover-on-time records as the cleanest reality check.

**Variation A (cautious end-user):**
> If handover risk is the deal-breaker, the alternative is ready secondary stock at the same price tier. I keep a comparable shortlist of those — reply "ready alternatives" and I'll send 5.

**Variation B (investor weighing risk):**
> Handover risk on this developer specifically is `{{risk_category}}` based on past projects. If you want the data, I'll send a 1-page reliability snapshot — 4 columns, no spin.

**Do not say this:**
> ~~"All off-plan is safe."~~ ~~"This developer always delivers."~~

## 7. "The service charge is too high."

**Reply:**
> Service charge variance is the most overlooked number on a listing. Three diagnostics on this building: (1) the YoY trend (last 5 years), (2) the reserve fund balance and contribution rate, (3) the major-works schedule. If any of the three look off, the high charge is a real problem. If all three are healthy, the charge is buying you a stable building. I'll send the 1-page OA snapshot tonight.

**Variation A (price-driven buyer):**
> If the OA snapshot confirms the high charge is structural, the right move is to negotiate the purchase price down by the present value of the gap to comparable buildings (typically AED 30–80K). I'll model that for you.

**Variation B (yield-driven investor):**
> Service charge directly compresses net yield. On this building, the gap to a comparable lower-charge building cuts net yield by `{{gap_pct}}` points. I'll send the side-by-side; you decide whether the building's other strengths justify the gap.

**Do not say this:**
> ~~"Service charges are normal at this level."~~ ~~"It's not that much."~~

## 8. "The currency / exchange rate is moving against me."

**Reply (international buyer):**
> The dirham is pegged to the USD; that part of the math is stable. What's moving is your home currency vs USD, which means the timing question is real. Two practical paths: (1) lock the deposit in USD now, finalize within 30 days; (2) split the payment plan into 3 tranches, hedging quarterly. I'll introduce you to a regulated FX desk that handles property transfers — they price the spread cleanly.

**Variation A (buyer worried about own currency weakness):**
> If your currency is at a 12-month low, waiting often costs more than acting (because the property side is independent of your FX). Send me your home currency and I'll send a 1-page sensitivity analysis covering ±10% FX moves vs property holding cost.

**Variation B (buyer waiting for a stronger currency):**
> Realistic FX targets: how much would your currency need to strengthen to matter? Most "wait for FX" buyers wait for a 5–10% move that historically takes 6–18 months. The property holding cost over that period typically eats half the FX gain. Run that math before waiting.

**Do not say this:**
> ~~"Don't worry about FX."~~ ~~"You'll lose money if you wait."~~

## 9. "I'm worried I won't get a mortgage."

**Reply:**
> Pre-approval is the cleanest answer to that worry — and it's free. I'll introduce you to a mortgage advisor (independent, not bank-employed) who can pre-approve in 7 working days and tell you exactly which banks fit your profile. With pre-approval in hand, your offer is taken seriously by sellers and your worry is removed.

**Variation A (self-employed buyer):**
> Self-employed mortgages in the UAE are tougher but absolutely available — typically 24 months of bank statements, 18 months of trade-license records, and 50–60% LTV (vs 75–80% for salaried). The advisor I work with has done 40+ self-employed cases.

**Variation B (buyer with credit concerns):**
> If credit is the worry, the pre-approval will surface it now without penalty — soft pull, no commitment. Better to know now than discover it during the SPA period.

**Do not say this:**
> ~~"You'll definitely get the mortgage."~~ ~~"Let's worry about that later."~~

## 10. "I'm not sure I'll get residency / a visa."

**Reply:**
> Property purchase in Dubai does NOT require residency or a visa — non-residents can buy freely in freehold areas. Separately, the Golden Visa pathway runs at AED 2M+ invested. Two scenarios: (1) you buy now without residency, no problem; (2) you buy at AED 2M+ and apply for the Golden Visa concurrently. I'll send the 1-page visa-pathway document with the current-year rules.

**Variation A (long-term plan buyer):**
> If the move is long-term (1–3 years), buying at the Golden Visa threshold is often the cleaner path because it stacks with the property purchase rather than running as a separate process.

**Variation B (uncertain about staying):**
> If you're uncertain whether you'll stay long-term, that affects the rent-vs-buy math more than it affects the visa question. Send me your most-likely-2-year-plan and I'll model rent-vs-buy honestly for both paths.

**Do not say this:**
> ~~"Don't worry about the visa."~~ ~~"Just buy and figure it out later."~~

## 11. "I'm worried about resale liquidity."

**Reply:**
> Liquidity is real and specific. On this building, last-90-day median DOM ran `{{dom_days}}` days — the comparable building benchmark for the corridor is `{{benchmark_dom}}`. If the gap is wide in either direction, that's the answer. I'll send the building-specific velocity report tonight.

**Variation A (long-hold investor):**
> If the hold is 5+ years, liquidity matters less than appreciation and yield. If the hold is 1–3 years, liquidity becomes the dominant question and we should restrict the shortlist to the 3 highest-velocity buildings in the area.

**Variation B (worried about thin buyer pool):**
> If the worry is buyer-pool depth, the diagnostic is simple: who buys this configuration in this area, and how many of them transact per quarter? I'll send the 1-page buyer-pool snapshot — small but honest sample.

**Do not say this:**
> ~~"This area always sells fast."~~ ~~"Liquidity isn't an issue."~~

## 12. "I don't trust the developer."

**Reply:**
> A specific worry deserves a specific answer. Three diagnostics on this developer: (1) on-time handover rate over the last 5 years, (2) refund-claim history (RERA-published), (3) any open legal cases on past projects. If any of these read poorly, walk away. If they read well, the worry doesn't apply to this developer specifically. I'll send the dossier.

**Variation A (buyer comparing two developers):**
> Reply "compare" and the developer name you're comparing against. I'll send a side-by-side reliability snapshot — 4 columns, no spin.

**Variation B (buyer with general off-plan worry):**
> If the worry isn't about this specific developer but about off-plan in general, the right answer might be ready secondary stock at a comparable price. Reply "ready alternatives" and I'll send 5.

**Do not say this:**
> ~~"This developer always delivers."~~ ~~"Don't read the reviews online."~~

## 13. "Saudi-specific: REGA / Sakani / financing questions."

**Reply (Arabic-friendly):**
> ترخيص ريقا متاح للعقار وتم التحقق منه. تمويل سكني ممكن من خلال البنوك المعتمدة (الراجحي، البلاد، الأهلي) بنسبة تمويل تصل إلى 90% للسعوديين. أرسل لك خلال اليوم: (1) رقم ترخيص ريقا، (2) قائمة البنوك المتاحة وأسعار التمويل التقديرية، (3) جدول زمني للتسجيل. إن أردت التحدث مع مستشار التمويل المباشر — أكتب "تمويل" وأرسل لك رقمه مباشرة.

**Variation A (Sakani-specific):**
> الدعم السكني يطبق على هذا العقار طالما يستوفي شروط الفئة المستهدفة. سأتأكد من سجل الدعم في حالتك عبر بوابة سكني خلال 24 ساعة، وأرسل لك التأكيد كتابيًا.

**Variation B (English-speaking buyer asking about KSA):**
> REGA license is verified for this property. Sakani financing is available for Saudi nationals through approved banks (Al Rajhi, Albilad, Alahli) at up to 90% LTV. I'll send the REGA license number, the bank list, and the indicative financing terms. The Sakani support program eligibility runs independently — if you want, I'll check eligibility through the Sakani portal within 24 hours.

**Do not say this:**
> ~~"REGA isn't required for this property."~~ ~~"Sakani is automatic."~~

## 14. "I viewed and went silent — sorry, busy week."

**Reply:**
> All good. Two paths from here: (1) you want me to keep the file open with no follow-up unless you message first, (2) you want a one-message summary of what changed in the area this week so you can re-engage on your own time. Reply "open" or "summary" — I'll do exactly that.

**Variation A (genuine quiet period):**
> Pace is your call. If you want, I'll send a weekly 3-line WhatsApp on the area you were looking at — no pitches, no chasing. Reply "weekly" or "no thanks".

**Variation B (silent because lost interest):**
> If the property went off your list, that's useful information for me too. Reply "lost" — I'll close the file cleanly and learn from it.

**Do not say this:**
> ~~"I was waiting to hear from you."~~ ~~"Are you still interested?"~~

## 15. "The seller wants too much. They won't budge."

**Reply (buyer-side):**
> Sellers who hold firm on month 2 often soften by month 4 — but the gap usually closes by 30–50% of the original discount, not 100%. If the gap to your number is over 8%, the deal probably won't close in the near term. If it's 3–6%, hold your number for 21 days; the seller's Salesforce shows the same calls I make. I'll route your written number with comparable transactions; if they don't move within 21 days, we restart.

**Reply (seller-side, when you're representing a stuck seller):**
> The buyer's number might not be the buyer's ceiling. Two things to test before walking: (1) the buyer's financing pre-approval level (not always equal to their offer), (2) the buyer's flexibility on timeline (a faster close often justifies a slightly stronger price). I'll WhatsApp the buyer's agent today with both questions — answers either way give us the negotiation map.

**Variation A (buyer-side, soft seller):**
> If the seller's only sticking point is the headline number, sometimes restructuring the deal — staggered payment, faster close, dropping a contingency — buys 2–4% in equivalent value. I'll model three structures and we'll send all three as written offers in parallel.

**Variation B (seller-side, hard buyer):**
> If the buyer truly cannot move, the cleanest path is to set a 21-day window. If they don't return within 21, we re-list with adjusted positioning (not necessarily price). I'll have the relisting plan ready before day 22.

**Do not say this:**
> ~~"The seller won't move, that's final."~~ ~~"Take it or leave it."~~
