# Appendix B — Bonus Onboarding Email Sequences

Two 5-email sequences. One for buyers, one for sellers. Both written in the same voice as the rest of the playbook: text-first, specific, no preamble.

---

## B.1 Buyer-side onboarding (5 emails)

### Buyer Email 1 — Welcome + how this will work
**Send:** Day 0 (immediately after first qualifying conversation)
**Subject:** What to expect from me, in 90 seconds

> `{{first_name}}`,
> 
> Welcome. Here's how this works on my side, so you know exactly what you're in for over the next 4–8 weeks:
> 
> 1. **No phone calls.** Everything is WhatsApp + email. If you specifically want a call, you tell me and I dial.
> 2. **One shortlist per week**, max 5 properties. If we don't find a fit in 4 weeks, the criteria change, not the volume.
> 3. **Every property comes with a document pack** — title deed, audited service-charge statement, comparable transactions. Always. You should never sign anything without these three.
> 4. **You decide pace.** I do not chase. If you go quiet for 14 days, I send one "still active or paused?" message and respect the answer.
> 
> One ask in return: when you reply to my messages, give me one of three signals — "send", "skip", or "park". Even one-word replies keep your file accurate and the next shortlist sharper.
> 
> I'll send your search-criteria capture form tomorrow.
> 
> — `{{agent_name}}`

### Buyer Email 2 — Search-criteria capture
**Send:** Day 1
**Subject:** 9 questions, 6 minutes — your search criteria

> `{{first_name}}`,
> 
> The shortlist is only as good as the criteria. Nine questions, takes 6 minutes. Reply in WhatsApp or email; numbered or full sentences both work.
> 
> 1. Max budget all-in (price + transfer fees + agent fee + first year service charge).
> 2. Cash, mortgage, or hybrid? If mortgage, are you pre-approved?
> 3. Areas you'd buy in — rank top 3.
> 4. Areas you'd never buy in — name 1–2.
> 5. Property type (apartment / townhouse / villa / penthouse).
> 6. Configuration (studio / 1BR / 2BR / 3BR / 4BR+).
> 7. Must-haves (3): the things that, if missing, the property is rejected.
> 8. Nice-to-haves (3): the things that would tip a close call.
> 9. Timeline: 30 days, 60 days, 90 days, or longer?
> 
> Send the answers. You'll have your first shortlist within 48 hours.
> 
> — `{{agent_name}}`

### Buyer Email 3 — Expectations: what's normal vs what's not
**Send:** Day 4
**Subject:** What's normal in a Dubai purchase, what's not
> `{{first_name}}`,
> 
> So you don't get spooked by the noise mid-process, here's what's normal and what's not.
> 
> **Normal:**
> - 5–8 viewings before a real shortlist of 2 emerges
> - The first offer is rejected; the second offer at 2–4% above is accepted; this is the standard rhythm
> - The seller's agent goes silent for 24–72 hours during negotiation; this is not a bad signal
> - The conveyancer flags 1–3 small SPA edits (clauses on snagging window, payment timing); this is healthy due diligence
> - 21–35 working days from offer-accepted to keys-in-hand for a cash deal; 35–60 for mortgage
> 
> **Not normal:**
> - An agent refusing to send the title deed scan before viewing
> - A seller refusing to share the audited service-charge statement
> - A developer (off-plan) refusing to disclose the RERA permit number
> - A pressure to "decide today"
> - Any agent saying "this is the only one of its kind" — there is always a similar one
> 
> If anything in the second list happens with another agent, walk. With me, it won't happen.
> 
> — `{{agent_name}}`

### Buyer Email 4 — What to bring to a viewing
**Send:** Day 7 (or day before first viewing)
**Subject:** What to actually look at in a viewing (it's not what you think)

> `{{first_name}}`,
> 
> Most viewings are spent looking at the wrong things. The 8 things actually worth checking, in order:
> 
> 1. **The kitchen window orientation** — does morning sun hit the counter? Does the window open?
> 2. **The laundry separation from the family wing** — if you can hear the washing machine from the master, you'll regret it in 6 months.
> 3. **Water stains under the AC units** — old or new? On all units or one?
> 4. **The master bathroom natural light** — windowed or just exhaust?
> 5. **The actual elevator wait time at peak** — visit at 8:15 a.m. on a weekday if possible.
> 6. **The neighbor immediately above and below** — if any, who lives there? Asking the building's security or a delivery driver outside often gives a faster answer than asking the agent.
> 7. **The view at 6 p.m., not at noon** — most listings photo at noon. The 6 p.m. read is the truthful one.
> 8. **The corridors outside the unit** — clean, well-lit, recently painted? This is a leading indicator of OA health.
> 
> If you can, bring a measuring tape and a phone-light. Take 25–40 photos, not 5. You'll re-look at them every night for two weeks before deciding.
> 
> — `{{agent_name}}`

### Buyer Email 5 — Before you sign
**Send:** Day 14 (or before SPA signing)
**Subject:** The 3-document check before you sign anything

> `{{first_name}}`,
> 
> We're approaching the SPA stage. Three documents to have on your desk *before* you sign:
> 
> 1. **The title deed scan** — must show seller name matching the SPA exactly, no liens or restrictions registered.
> 2. **The audited service-charge statement (last 2 years)** — confirms the rate, the reserve fund balance, and any qualifications in the audit.
> 3. **The OA's 5-year major-works schedule** — tells you what's coming and at what cost. Not always available, but ask in writing.
> 
> Plus, three things to confirm in writing from the seller:
> - The handover-key timeline (specific date)
> - The snagging window length and process
> - Any furniture/appliances included or excluded (specific list)
> 
> One more: choose your conveyancer *now*, not when you get to the SPA. I work with two; I'll send their fee sheets so you choose. Pre-engaging your conveyancer means they read the SPA in 24 hours, not 5 days.
> 
> Reply when you're ready to read the SPA together.
> 
> — `{{agent_name}}`

## B.2 Seller-side onboarding (5 emails)

### Seller Email 1 — Welcome + the pricing reality
**Send:** Day 0 (immediately after listing agreement)
**Subject:** Pricing reality — read this before we list
> `{{seller_first_name}}`,
> 
> Welcome. Before we list, three honest paragraphs about pricing.
> 
> The price you list at sets the days-on-market and the eventual close price. List 8% above market, your DOM doubles, and the close price ends up 4–6% below the realistic-market number. List at the realistic-market number, you close in 6 weeks at the asking. List 3% below, you close in 3 weeks at or slightly above asking with multiple offers.
> 
> This is not theory. I've watched it on 40+ listings.
> 
> The realistic-market number for `{{property}}` is `{{realistic_price}}` — based on `{{comp_count}}` comparable transactions in the last 90 days. The audited number, not the brochure number, is what your buyer's bank will value against. Anchoring above it costs you weeks and money.
> 
> If you want to list higher anyway because of personal anchoring, that's your call — I'll do it once, and after 30 days of no offers, we re-price together.
> 
> Reply to confirm the listing price and we'll go live within 48 hours.
> 
> — `{{agent_name}}`

### Seller Email 2 — Photos day prep
**Send:** Day 2
**Subject:** Photos day — what changes the listing's click-through rate
> `{{seller_first_name}}`,
> 
> Photos drive 70% of the inquiry rate on Bayut and Property Finder. Three things to do before the photographer arrives:
> 
> 1. **Declutter to a magazine-shoot level.** Counters clear except 1 plant. Beds made tight. Floors mopped. Windows cleaned (the natural light reads completely differently).
> 2. **Open every curtain and blind.** Even ones you usually close. We're shooting for max natural light.
> 3. **Remove personal items.** Family photos, religious items, anything that gives the buyer a reason to project "this is someone else's home" rather than "this could be my home". This is uncomfortable but it's a real conversion lever.
> 
> The photographer arrives `{{photo_date}}` at `{{photo_time}}`. Shoot takes 90 minutes. Final photos in the listing within 48 hours.
> 
> — `{{agent_name}}`

### Seller Email 3 — The first 14 days plan
**Send:** Day 5 (just before going live)
**Subject:** Days 0–14 — the listing's first two weeks
> `{{seller_first_name}}`,
> 
> The first 14 days set the trajectory of the entire listing. Here's the structured plan:
> 
> **Days 0–3 (the surge):**
> - Listing live on Bayut, Property Finder, Dubizzle.
> - Featured-listing budget allocated to the top 25% of similar listings in the corridor.
> - WhatsApp blast to my qualified buyer list (the warm 80, not all 800).
> - Expect 30–60 inquiries; I qualify down to 8–14 real ones in 72 hours.
> 
> **Days 3–10 (the viewings):**
> - 6–10 viewings if the price is right.
> - First offer typically lands by day 7. We don't accept the first offer; we use it to triangulate the realistic close.
> 
> **Days 10–14 (the calibration):**
> - If we have 1–2 offers, we negotiate.
> - If we have 0 offers, we re-look at the listing copy, the photos, and the price — in that order. Price is the *last* lever, not the first.
> 
> If days 14–30 also show zero offers, we step back and re-strategize. I'll never let a listing drift silently for 60 days; we agree on action steps every 14 days.
> 
> — `{{agent_name}}`

### Seller Email 5 — Negotiation strategy when the first offer arrives
**Send:** Day of first offer
**Subject:** First offer is in. Here's how I want to handle it.
> `{{seller_first_name}}`,
> 
> First offer received: AED `{{offer_amount}}`, conditions: `{{conditions}}`.
> 
> Three things matter in how we respond:
> 
> 1. **The gap to your number.** `{{gap_pct}}`% below your asking. The historical pattern: gaps under 5% close in 1 round; gaps 5–9% close in 2 rounds; gaps over 9% rarely close at all unless one side moves materially.
> 
> 2. **The buyer's profile.** `{{buyer_profile}}`. This profile typically has `{{negotiation_room}}` AED of additional movement based on what their pre-approval suggests.
> 
> 3. **The conditions.** Cash close vs financing close vs financing-with-contingency are three different deals at the same headline number. We adjust our counter accordingly.
> 
> My recommended counter: AED `{{counter_amount}}` with a `{{counter_terms}}` term. This signals seriousness without giving up the negotiation runway.
> 
> Reply "go" to send the counter, or any adjustment you want to the number or terms. I'll route within 2 hours of your reply.
> 
> — `{{agent_name}}`
