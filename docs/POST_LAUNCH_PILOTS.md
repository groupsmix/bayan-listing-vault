# Post-Launch Pilot Program

This document outlines the pilot program for Bayan, to be run **after** the initial launch (Days 1–14). Pilots are not a launch blocker — they are a post-launch feedback loop to refine the product for the next 100 buyers.

---

## Why Pilots Post-Launch?

**Rationale:** Running pilots before launch delays revenue and creates a dependency on external feedback before the product is validated. The mandate is to ship v1 and take money first, then iterate based on real buyer feedback.

**Pilot timing:** Days 1–6 of the launch playbook (see `docs/LAUNCH_DAY_BY_DAY.md`) include a mini-pilot wave of 10 agents who receive free early access in exchange for feedback. This is sufficient to catch major issues before public launch on Day 7.

**Full pilot program:** If you want to run a more structured pilot program (20–50 agents, formal feedback sessions, co-creation workshops), do it in Month 2–3 after the initial launch wave. By then, you'll have 50–100 paying buyers, real usage data, and a validated product to iterate on.

---

## Pilot Program Structure (Month 2–3)

### Goal

Collect deep qualitative feedback from 20–50 agents who use Bayan daily for 30 days. Identify the 3–5 highest-impact improvements for the next major update.

---

### Pilot Recruitment

**Who to recruit:**
- Agents who bought Bayan in the first 30 days and used it actively (check Gumroad purchase date + follow-up DMs)
- Agents who requested custom prompts or sequences (they're engaged and have specific needs)
- Agents in underrepresented markets (Sharjah, Northern Emirates, Jeddah, Dammam) to expand coverage

**Recruitment DM:**

> [First name] — you bought Bayan [X] days ago and I've seen you using it actively.
>
> I'm running a 30-day pilot program in [Month] to co-create the next major update. 20 agents, 1 feedback session per week, free access to all new content before it ships.
>
> Interested? Reply "pilot" and I'll send the details.

**Incentive:**
- Free access to all Month 2–6 updates (normally covered by the 12-month update window, but pilots get early access)
- Co-creation credit: "Pilot contributors: [Name], [Name], [Name]" in the next PDF update
- Optional: AED 500 Gumroad credit toward team licensing if they refer 3+ agents who buy

---

### Pilot Structure

**Duration:** 30 days

**Commitment:**
- Use Bayan daily for your own listings, follow-ups, and captions
- Attend 1 weekly 30-minute Zoom feedback session (4 sessions total)
- Submit 1 written feedback form at the end of 30 days

**Weekly Zoom Sessions:**

**Week 1: Onboarding + First Impressions**
- What did you use first?
- What worked immediately?
- What felt clunky or unclear?

**Week 2: Listing Prompts Deep Dive**
- Which prompts did you use most?
- Which prompts did you skip or rewrite heavily?
- What property types or price tiers are missing?

**Week 3: Follow-Ups + Objections**
- Which follow-up sequences did you use?
- Which objection scripts landed with buyers?
- What objections are missing from the playbook?

**Week 4: Social Captions + Master Voice Prompt**
- Which captions got the most engagement?
- Did the master voice prompt work as expected?
- What would you change about the voice training process?

---

### Feedback Collection

**Written Feedback Form (end of 30 days):**

1. **Most valuable section:** Which section of Bayan saved you the most time?
2. **Least valuable section:** Which section did you skip or ignore?
3. **Missing content:** What property type, market, or use case is missing?
4. **Biggest friction point:** What was the hardest part of using Bayan?
5. **One thing to change:** If you could change one thing about Bayan, what would it be?
6. **Would you recommend it?** Yes / No / Maybe. Why?

**Incentive for completing the form:** AED 100 Gumroad credit (can be used toward team licensing or gifted to another agent).

---

### Pilot Outputs

**Deliverables from the pilot program:**

1. **Top 5 requested features** (ranked by frequency across all pilots)
2. **Top 3 friction points** (what makes Bayan hard to use)
3. **Top 3 missing property types or markets** (what coverage gaps exist)
4. **Testimonials** (2-sentence reactions from pilots who found it valuable)
5. **Case studies** (1–2 pilots who used Bayan to close a deal or double their listing output)

**Use these outputs to:**
- Prioritize the Month 3–4 content update
- Rewrite the weakest 10 prompts
- Add 20 new prompts for the most-requested property types
- Update the sales page with pilot testimonials and case studies

## Alternative: Async Pilot (No Zoom Sessions)

If you don't want to run weekly Zoom sessions, run an async pilot:

**Structure:**
- Recruit 50 pilots (larger cohort, lower commitment)
- No Zoom sessions
- 1 written feedback form at the end of 30 days
- Incentive: AED 50 Gumroad credit (lower than sync pilot)

**Pros:**
- Scales to more pilots
- No time commitment for weekly Zooms
- Easier to recruit (lower barrier to entry)

**Cons:**
- Lower engagement (expect 30–40% completion rate vs 80% for sync pilot)
- Less qualitative depth (no live discussion)
- Harder to build community among pilots

---

## Pilot Program Not a Launch Blocker

**Reminder:** The pilot program is a post-launch optimization, not a pre-launch requirement. The mandate is to ship v1 and take money first. Run the pilot program in Month 2–3 after you have 50–100 paying buyers and real usage data.

**If you skip the pilot program entirely:** You can still iterate based on buyer feedback from DMs, emails, and Gumroad messages. The pilot program is a structured way to collect feedback, but it's not the only way.
